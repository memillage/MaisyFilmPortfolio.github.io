About Me
I am Maisy Millage, a recent graduate from Iowa State University with a passion for filmmaking. I have been captivated by the power of storytelling from a young age. Even before understanding the technical aspects of filmmaking, I would write stories and envision them as visual narratives, complete with composition and camera movements. Though the thought of creating films still seemed like a far-off fantasy given the cost of producing a film.
While I attended high school, technological advancements made camera equipment more affordable and enabled phone cameras to capture high-definition footage. This shift caused my once-distant dream of creating quality films to become an attainable reality. It motivated me to immerse myself in the world of filmmaking through countless hours of YouTube videos. I absorbed knowledge of filmmaking concepts, lighting techniques, camera settings, and the latest advancements in camera technology. A few years later, during Covid-19, I shot a few short films for at-home competitions with my trusty iPhone. A few months later, my parents gifted me a remarkable tool that elevated my filmmaking journey: the Blackmagic Pocket Cinema Camera 6k. This gift allowed me to translate the knowledge I acquired from hours of watching videos into tangible creations that showcased my vision and storytelling abilities.
When choosing a college major, I decided to pursue Computer Engineering. I saw it as a path that could equip me with valuable skills applicable to filmmaking, like problem-solving, and a solid foundation in technology, especially given the increasing reliance on technology like CGI in the industry. After going through school, I still hold this belief to be true.
